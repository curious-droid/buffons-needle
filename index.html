The Math Behind Buffon's Needle

Buffon's needle is a probability method for estimating the value of \( \pi \). The experiment involves dropping a needle of a certain length onto a floor with equally spaced parallel lines. The probability \( P \) that the needle will cross a line is given by:

\( P = \frac{2L}{\pi D} \)

where:

  • L is the length of the needle.
  • D is the distance between the lines.
  • \( \pi \) is the value of Pi we want to estimate.

By rearranging the formula, we can estimate \( \pi \) as:

\( \pi \approx \frac{2L \times \text{Number of drops}}{\text{Number of hits} \times D} \)

As you drop more needles, the estimate of \( \pi \) should converge to the actual value.
